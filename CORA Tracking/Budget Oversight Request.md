## Note
Trustees do not need CORA to request records from staff — they can direct the TA to produce these documents. However, a formal CORA request creates a legal deadline (3 business days) and a paper trail if records are denied or delayed. Belt and suspenders.

## Items Requested (9 total)

1. **Cost allocation plan/methodology** — How admin costs are split across enterprise funds. If none exists, a written confirmation of that fact.

2. **Admin timekeeping records** — Time logs showing how admin employees split time between General Fund and enterprise fund work (FY-2024, FY-2025).

3. **Interfund transfer ledger** — All transfers between General Fund and enterprise funds (FY-2022 through FY-2025 YTD), with dates, amounts, purpose, and authorization.

4. **Consolidated admin cost schedule** — Total admin costs across ALL funds, by line item and fund (FY-2024, FY-2025). The number the budget never shows in one place.

5. **Rate study basis** — The analysis used to set water/sewer rates, including admin overhead assumptions. Also any CWRPDA rate covenant compliance analysis.

6. **DWRF loan compliance reports** — All progress reports submitted to CWRPDA for Phase 1 water projects since loan approval (March 2025).

7. **New recurring cost schedule** — Every subscription, lease, cellular plan, and new position added since Jan 2023, with annual cost, fund charged, and contract term.

8. **Wynn employment contract** — Complete contract with amendments (redundant with Pete's request — intentional).

9. **Wynn payroll records by fund** — Complete compensation from all funds for 2023-2025, broken out by fund source (redundant with Pete's request — intentional).

## What We're Looking For
- Items 1-2: Does a cost allocation methodology actually exist? If not, enterprise fund charges are arbitrary.
- Item 3: Have there been transfers the Board didn't authorize?
- Item 4: What's the REAL total cost of administration? (Budget shows ~$290K when you piece it together across 4 pages)
- Item 5: Are ratepayers subsidizing admin bloat through inflated water rates?
- Item 6: Is Phase 1 on track, or are there cost overruns?
- Item 7: How fast are recurring costs growing?
- Items 8-9: Redundancy with Pete's request is deliberate — harder to delay two requesters.
