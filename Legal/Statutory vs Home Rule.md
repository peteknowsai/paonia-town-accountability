# Statutory vs Home Rule

This is the single most important legal distinction in the initiative fight.

## Paonia's Status
- Organized under **C.R.S. § 31-1-203** as a **statutory municipality**
- **NOT** a home rule municipality

## Why It Matters
- Home rule cities have broad powers to limit the initiative process through their charters
- Statutory towns must follow **state law** strictly
- Statutory towns have **less** discretion to reject initiatives, not more
- The initiative power in statutory towns derives from statute, not from local charter

## Buchner's Error
[[Clay Buchner]]'s legal analysis of the September 2025 initiatives relied on authorities distinguishing home rule powers — cases like *Vagneur v. Aspen* (a home rule city). Those limitations **do not apply** to Paonia.

This is either:
1. A fundamental legal error (incompetence), or
2. A deliberate misapplication of law to justify a predetermined outcome (bad faith)

Either way, it's the foundation of every initiative rejection and it's wrong.
