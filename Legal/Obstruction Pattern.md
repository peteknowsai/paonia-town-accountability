# Documented Obstruction Pattern

The Town of Paonia has engaged in a systematic pattern of obstructing citizen democracy while expanding government surveillance.

## 1. False Initiative Rejection (May 25, 2025)
[[Samira Vetter]] rejected STR initiative claiming it would "establish fees" when text explicitly eliminated them. Backed by [[Clay Buchner]]'s legal analysis misapplying home rule law to a statutory town.

## 2. Illegal Executive Session (June 10, 2025)
Board convened executive session to discuss "Initiative Petitions" — already decided, doesn't fit any authorized purpose under C.R.S. § 24-6-402(4). Using executive session to strategize against citizen petitions violates Colorado Open Meetings Law.

## 3. Trustee Removal (August 15, 2024)
[[Bill Brunner]] removed 4-1 after [[Stefen Wynn]] conditioned his continued employment on Brunner's removal. Brunner's offense: exercising oversight. Chilled all remaining democratic participation.

## 4. Surveillance Without Process
- $50,000 Verkada camera system approved without public hearing on privacy implications
- Daxbot robots deployed June 2025 without advance notice or public hearing
- Town claims no resources for initiative processing while spending freely on surveillance

## 5. Records Obstruction
CORA requests for Microsoft 365 audit logs denied — claimed "no responsive records." Technically impossible; M365 generates logs automatically and they cannot be fully disabled.

1. **C.R.C.P. Rule 106(a)(4)** — judicial review of abuse of discretion
2. **C.R.C.P. Rule 106(a)(2)** — mandamus compelling acceptance
3. **42 U.S.C. § 1983** — civil rights (First Amendment petition rights)
4. **18 U.S.C. § 241** — conspiracy against rights
5. **18 U.S.C. § 242** — deprivation of rights under color of law
6. Criminal referral to DA and AG
