# Clay Buchner

**Role:** Town Attorney, Paonia

## The Problem
Buchner's core belief, stated in his July 2, 2025 memo:

> "Imagine if citizen-initiated provisions could eliminate fees for administrative services provided by local government, the result would be bankruptcy of the Town."

Translation: democracy is fine as long as it doesn't affect revenue.

## Pagosa Springs History (2020-2023)

### TIF Initiative (2020)
- Voters approved 3-to-1 requiring voter approval for TIF subsidies over $1M
- Buchner researched ways for the town to sue its own voters to overturn it
- Eventually advised against litigation, but the research reveals his mindset

### STR Fee Debacle (2022)
- Voters approved $150/month/bedroom workforce housing fee on STRs
- STR owners sued
- Dec 26, 2022: Judge Wilson struck down voter-approved fees
- Dec 28, 2022: Judge Wilson reversed himself, calling his own order an "error"
- Buchner failed to capitalize on this gift
- Per Pagosa Daily Post: "Clay Buchner failed to effectively defend the desires of the Town voters"
- **Result: Replaced as town attorney**

## Current Misconduct in Paonia
- Misapplying home rule law to a **statutory town** — fundamental legal error
- Using cases like *Vagneur v. Aspen* (home rule) to justify rejecting Paonia initiatives
- "Bankruptcy" doctrine would eliminate citizen power over fees entirely

## Relevance to Initiatives
- Will likely argue camera ban is administrative, not legislative
- Will likely argue robot moratorium conflicts with ADA compliance
- Both arguments are preemptively addressed in cover letters
- His Pagosa Springs record will be cited if adequacy of legal analysis is challenged
