## Summary
Temporary moratorium on autonomous robots in public spaces. Expires December 31, 2028. Explicit ADA carve-out. Encourages Town to develop regulatory framework through public process. Voter-only amendment/repeal.

## v3 Changes (from v2)
- **Privacy-led recitals** — right to move through town without being surveilled by autonomous machines
- **Tighter definition** — must be capable of collecting/recording/transmitting data about persons; excludes RC toys and hobby equipment
- **Deleted Section 5(c)** — the "self-destruct" clause that said if no framework adopted, moratorium expires with no regulation. Town could have just run out the clock.
- **Added Amendment and Repeal section** — voter-only, matching camera ban and STR. Board can't quietly repeal.

## Key Legal Anchors
- Temporary moratoria are well-established legislative acts in Colorado (C.R.S. § 29-20-104)
- *Vagneur* — liberal construction of initiative power
- No state regulatory framework for sidewalk robots (SB 20-092 died in 2020)

# FULL TEXT: ORDINANCE

## ORDINANCE NO. 2026-__

**AN INITIATED ORDINANCE OF THE PEOPLE OF THE TOWN OF PAONIA, COLORADO, ESTABLISHING A MORATORIUM ON AUTONOMOUS ROBOTS IN PUBLIC SPACES, PROTECTING PUBLIC SAFETY AND PRIVACY, AND PROVIDING FOR ENFORCEMENT AND REVIEW**

### RECITALS

**WHEREAS**, the Town of Paonia is a statutory municipality organized under C.R.S. § 31-1-203; and

**WHEREAS**, C.R.S. §§ 31-15-101 and 31-15-401 grant statutory municipalities police-power authority to protect the health, safety, and welfare of its citizens; and

**WHEREAS**, residents of Paonia have a fundamental right to move through their own town without being surveilled, recorded, or tracked by autonomous machines they did not consent to and cannot opt out of; and

**WHEREAS**, autonomous robots equipped with cameras, sensors, and wireless connectivity operating on public sidewalks and streets constitute mobile surveillance platforms capable of recording the movements, associations, and daily routines of every person they encounter; and

**WHEREAS**, in a community of approximately 1,500 residents, such technology eliminates the practical anonymity and personal privacy that have historically characterized life in a small, self-governing town; and

**WHEREAS**, in June 2025, autonomous camera-equipped robots manufactured by Daxbot, Inc. were deployed on Paonia streets and sidewalks without advance public notice through established channels, without a dedicated public hearing on the technology, and without opportunity for citizen input on the deployment; and

**WHEREAS**, the robots were equipped with cameras, GPS, laser sensors, and cellular connectivity, and the data collection capabilities, data storage and retention practices, and third-party data sharing arrangements were not disclosed to the public before, during, or after deployment; and

**WHEREAS**, the robot manufacturer, Daxbot, Inc., produces both ADA assessment robots and autonomous security patrol robots on a substantially similar hardware platform, and this dual-use nature of the technology was not disclosed to the public; and

**WHEREAS**, no formal safety assessment was conducted or published prior to operating autonomous machines on public sidewalks shared with pedestrians, children, elderly residents, and persons with disabilities; and

**WHEREAS**, Colorado has no regulatory framework governing autonomous robots on public sidewalks, as the only proposed legislation (SB 20-092, 2020) was postponed indefinitely and never became law; and

**WHEREAS**, the People of Paonia have the right to require a public process before data-collecting autonomous technology is deployed in their community;

**NOW, THEREFORE, BE IT ORDAINED BY THE PEOPLE OF THE TOWN OF PAONIA, COLORADO:**

### Section 1. Title.

This initiated ordinance shall be known and cited as the "Autonomous Robot Moratorium."

### Section 2. Definitions.

For purposes of this ordinance:

(a) **"Autonomous robot"** means any self-propelled mechanical device that operates in a public space without continuous direct human control and that is capable of collecting, recording, or transmitting data about persons or their activities. This includes ground-based, aerial, and aquatic devices. It does not include personal consumer devices such as remote-controlled toys or hobby equipment that lack data collection capabilities directed at third parties.

(b) **"Public space"** means any area accessible to the general public, including streets, sidewalks, parks, trails, alleys, and public rights-of-way.

(c) **"Continuous direct human control"** means real-time operation by a human operator who directs all movement and functions of the device throughout its operation, as distinguished from pre-programmed or algorithmic navigation.

### Section 3. Moratorium.

(a) **Prohibition.** During the moratorium period, no person or entity shall operate an autonomous robot in any public space within the Town limits.

(b) **No fees or permits.** This ordinance does not establish, require, or authorize any fees, licenses, or permits.

(c) **Exception.** Robots deployed by a public safety agency in active response to a specific emergency, under continuous direct human control by a trained operator, are exempt from this prohibition.

### Section 4. Federal Compliance.

Nothing in this ordinance shall be construed to prohibit or impede the Town's compliance with the Americans with Disabilities Act or any other federal or state law. The Town may fulfill any such obligation using methods that do not require autonomous robots.

### Section 5. Moratorium Period.

(a) The moratorium established by this ordinance expires on December 31, 2028.

(b) Prior to expiration, the Town is encouraged to develop a regulatory framework for autonomous robots through a public process that includes public notice, public hearings, and opportunity for citizen comment.

### Section 6. Enforcement.

(a) Violations of this ordinance constitute a civil infraction.

(b) Any registered elector of the Town may report a violation to the Town.

(c) Any registered elector of the Town may seek injunctive relief in Delta County District Court to enforce this ordinance. A prevailing plaintiff shall be entitled to reasonable attorney fees and costs.

### Section 7. Amendment and Repeal.

This ordinance may only be amended or repealed by vote of the registered electors of the Town.

### Section 8. Severability.

If any provision of this ordinance is held invalid by a court of competent jurisdiction, the remaining provisions shall continue in full force and effect.

### Section 9. Effective Date.

This ordinance takes effect thirty (30) days after certification of election results.
