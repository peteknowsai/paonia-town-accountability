## What It Does
Temporary moratorium on autonomous robots in public spaces. Expires December 31, 2028. Encourages the Town to develop a regulatory framework through public process before then.

## Key Definitions
- **Autonomous robot** — self-propelled device operating without continuous direct human control (ground, aerial, aquatic)
- **Continuous direct human control** — real-time operation directing all movement, not pre-programmed/algorithmic navigation

## Exceptions
- Emergency response robots under continuous human control

## Key Provisions
- No fees, licenses, or permits created
- Explicit ADA compliance carve-out (Section 4) — Town can fulfill ADA obligations using non-robotic methods
- Moratorium auto-expires if Town doesn't adopt regulations
- Citizens can seek injunctive relief with attorney fees

## The Trigger
June 17-18, 2025: Daxbot robots deployed on Paonia streets for ADA sidewalk assessment contracted to Mile High Accessibility Consultants (MHAC) for $33,500.

### What Went Wrong
- **No advance notice** — Facebook post day-of, Delta County Sheriff not notified
- **No public hearing** on the technology (board discussed cost, not robots/privacy)
- **No privacy disclosure** — cameras, GPS, lasers, cellular connectivity, no data handling policy
- **Dual-use platform undisclosed** — Daxbot makes security patrol robots on same hardware
- **No safety assessment** — autonomous machines on sidewalks with kids and elderly
- **No data use policy** — nothing prevents imagery from being used for any purpose

### What the Town Got Right
- ADA compliance is federally mandated and was 35 years overdue
- $33,500 was lowest of 7 bids
- Robots produced engineering-grade data (6.85 miles of sidewalk, 116 curb ramps)
- Town followed through with ADA transition plan drafts by Q3 2025

### ADA Is Not a Defense Against This Initiative
- ADA requires transition plans, not robots
- 6 of 7 bidders used human assessment methods
- Moratorium doesn't prevent ADA compliance
