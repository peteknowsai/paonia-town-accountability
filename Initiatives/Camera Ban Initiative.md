## What It Does
Prohibits the Town from installing, operating, or maintaining surveillance cameras on public property. Requires removal of existing Verkada cameras within 90 days.

## Exceptions
- Body-worn cameras on law enforcement
- Warrant-authorized cameras for specific investigations
- Public meeting live-streaming cameras

## Key Provisions
- Contracts violating the ordinance are void
- Citizens can seek injunctive relief with attorney fees
- Knowing violation = official misconduct
- Can only be amended or repealed by voter approval

## The Trigger
Town approved $50,000 Verkada surveillance camera contract without meaningful public input. Verkada has a documented history:
- 150,000 cameras hacked in 2021 (hardcoded credentials)
- Employees used facial recognition to harass female coworkers
- $2.95M FTC fine for security failures
- 100+ employees including interns had super admin access to all customer feeds

## Legal Defense
- Clearly legislative under Blackwell test
- Paonia is statutory (not home rule) — state law governs
- Cover letter puts Town on notice re: prior obstruction pattern
- Enumerated legal consequences if obstructed (Rule 106, mandamus, § 1983, criminal referral, FBI referral)
