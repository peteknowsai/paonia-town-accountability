# Daxbot Dual-Use Technology

## Company
- **Daxbot, Inc.** — Oregon-based
- Originally a delivery robot startup (StartEngine crowdfunding)
- Now markets as "Urban Service Robots"

## Two Product Lines, Same Platform
Both built on the "Helium-class" hardware platform:

### ADA Assessment Robots (deployed in Paonia)
- Sidewalk slope measurement
- Curb ramp evaluation
- Photogrammetry mapping
- Data integrated into ArcGIS

### Security Patrol Robots
- Autonomous patrol coverage
- Live video monitoring by "24/7 US-based operators"
- Incident detection and "confirmed-only escalation"
- Evidence capture for law enforcement
- Deterrence capability
- Quote from site: "people see them, they're less likely to do certain things"

## Shared Capabilities
- Cameras
- Centimeter-accurate GPS (RTK)
- Laser sensors
- Cellular connectivity
- Remote operator monitoring
- Collision detection
- 8-12 hour battery
- All-terrain treads
- 3 feet tall, 2 feet wide, up to 4 mph

## Why This Matters
The Town never disclosed that the robots were built by a company that also makes surveillance robots on the same hardware. The ADA robots are technically capable of collecting far more data than sidewalk measurements. And the Town has published no policy governing what happens with that data.
