# Wynn Compensation

## The Fund Shift

Stefen Wynn holds both **Town Administrator** and **Town Treasurer** titles (Resolution 08-2023, July 11, 2023).

His compensation shifted dramatically from the General Fund to enterprise funds between FY-2023 and FY-2024, with no visible Board authorization:

| Fund | Account | FY-2023 | FY-2024 | FY-2025 | FY-2026 |
|------|---------|---------|---------|---------|---------|
| General Fund | 10-41-02 | $153,114 | $45,376 | $35,000 | $33,000 |
| Water | 60-50-02 | $0 | $35,534 | $56,000 | $48,600 |
| Wastewater | 70-51-02 | $0 | $35,534 | $47,500 | $48,600 |
| Sanitation | 80-52-02 | $330 | $4,718 | $6,500 | $9,400 |
| **TOTAL** | | **$153,444** | **$121,163** | **$145,000** | **$139,600** |

### What Happened
- FY-2023: Paid almost entirely from General Fund ($153K)
- FY-2024: General Fund dropped to $45K; $75K appeared in enterprise funds
- FY-2025-2026: General Fund ~$33K; enterprise funds ~$107-112K
- **The compensation didn't shrink — it shifted**

### What's Missing
- ❌ No Board resolution authorizing the reallocation
- ❌ No documented cost allocation methodology
- ❌ No timekeeping records showing enterprise fund work
- ❌ No public discussion of the shift in any meeting minutes reviewed
- ❌ The total across all funds is never presented as a single figure in the budget

### How We Found This
The budget shows 4 separate line items across 4 different pages (102, 126, 132, 138). You have to manually piece together all four to see the total. The FY-2026 budget also has a second view (pages 236-245) showing what appear to be prior-year comparison figures.

## The Employment Contract
- Negotiated in **executive session** on June 27, 2023
- Board voted to "accept the contract as presented" and authorize Mayor to sign
- No compensation terms discussed in public session
- **Resolution 08-2023** (July 11, 2023) appoints him to both roles but specifies NO salary, NO terms, NO fund allocation
- The actual contract is not in any public meeting packet reviewed

## The Conflict
He is the Town Administrator who **prepares the budget** that determines **his own salary allocation** across funds. The Board approves the budget as a whole, but the line-item detail is spread across multiple pages and never totaled.

## Enterprise Fund Siphon Concern
Jeremiah Garcia (local with direct town experience) flagged this pattern:
- Admin costs allocated to enterprise funds = **one-way siphon**
- Ratepayers subsidize General Fund administration through water/sewer bills
- No documented methodology = no accountability
- Water Fund paying $48,600/yr in "Trustee/Admin Salaries" while Water/WW has only 3.0 FTE of its own operational staff
