# Budget Analysis

Comprehensive fiscal analysis completed March 23, 2026. Full report: `~/Projects/zero/data/paonia-fiscal-analysis.pdf`

## Key Findings

### Grant Dependency
- Only **31% of total revenue** comes from the town's own operations
- 34% grants, 35% loans = **69% external funding**
- Total FY-2026 appropriations: **$16.16M** on a $1.66M General Fund revenue base

### Personnel Growth
| Year | FTE | Personnel Cost | Cost/Resident |
|------|-----|---------------|---------------|
| FY-2023 | 19.0 | $1.83M | $1,259 |
| FY-2024 | 22.5 | $1.82M | $1,247 |
| FY-2025 | 23.5 | $2.17M | $1,486 |
| FY-2026 | 25.7 | $2.42M | $1,646 |

- **35% more staff** in 3 years; population grew **1.1%**
- Admin grew 57.5% (4.0 → 6.3 FTE) — fastest of any department

### Recurring Cost Trap
New annual obligations since 2023: ~$950K-1M/year
- Verkada cloud licenses, cellular meters, street sweeper lease, patrol car program, new staff, SaaS subscriptions

### Debt
- Known debt: **~$9.35M**
- Annual debt service: **$822K** (up 149% from FY-2024)
- New $5.26M CWRPDA loan through **2045**

### Fund Balance Drawdown
- FY-2026: Starting $5.40M → Ending $4.14M (**-23.4%**)
- Sales Tax CIP drops to $16K (from $558K)
- Bridge Reserve drops 73.5%

## Water System Crisis
- 2019 boil order and emergency declaration
- Phase 1 CIP: $6.2M (tank relining + Western Loop waterline). Mostly not started as of Q1 2025.
- Phases 2-5: **unfunded**, likely $15-30M+
- Historic drought: Lake Powell at 43% of normal inflows, 80%+ SNOTEL stations in snow drought

## The Embezzlement Precedent
- 2005-2013: $480,000 embezzled by Kirstin Chesnik
- "No controls were in place. Review of virtually any bank statement would have revealed the theft."
- Town was paying expenses with envelopes of cash without receipts
