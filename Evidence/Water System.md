# Water System

## Timeline of Crisis
- **Early 1900s**: Much of spring and conveyance infrastructure built. Some never upgraded.
- **February 14, 2019**: Catastrophic water loss event
- **February 18, 2019**: State issues **Boil Order**. Town declares emergency. Water shut off.
- **January 7, 2020**: Referendum passes establishing water tap moratorium (still in effect)
- **2020-2023**: Sanitary survey deficiencies, CDPHE compliance issues
- **2024**: 9,000 linear feet of steel pipes slated for replacement. Delayed.
- **March 7, 2025**: $9.74M DWRF loan approved for Phase 1
- **Q1 2025**: Tank relining site work ~98% complete. 5 change orders already.
- **August 2025**: Water restrictions — not for drought, but main tank offline for construction
- **FY-2026**: Phase 1 continues. Phases 2-5 unfunded.

## What's Actually Been Completed (as of Q1 2025)
- ✅ Site prep for 2MG tank relining (~98% of site work)
- ✅ DWRF loan approved ($9.74M including $3M principal forgiveness)
- ✅ EIAF Grant secured ($956K for tank relining)
- ✅ Hydrogeological study (preliminary findings — more water than assumed)
- ⏳ Tank relining itself — NOT STARTED (competitive bidding pending)
- ⏳ Western Loop waterline replacement — NOT STARTED
- ⏳ Raw water metering — NOT STARTED
- ⏳ Cellular meter replacement — in progress ($50K)
- ❌ Phases 2-5 of 20-year CIP — NOT FUNDED
- ❌ Wastewater system assessment — INCOMPLETE

## The DWRF Loan Structure
- $3,744,000 Leveraged Loan (subsidized market rate)
- $1,344,609 Base Direct Loan (1.50% / 20 years)
- $1,655,391 BIL General Supplemental (1.50% / 20 years)
- $3,000,000 BIL Principal Forgiveness
- Annual payment: ~$263,654 through 2045
- **Condition**: Rates must cover Authority's rate covenant

## Drought Context (2026)
- Lake Powell inflows: **43% of normal** (April-July forecast)
- 80%+ SNOTEL stations in snow drought (Jan 2026)
- Colorado Sun: water officials describe forecasts as **"grim"**
- Paonia's water from mountain springs — directly vulnerable

## How to Get Water Project Records
1. CORA request quarterly TA reports (project status + change orders)
2. CORA request Water Fund capital outlay invoices (accounts 60-50-64 through 60-50-69)
3. Request DWRF loan compliance reports from CWRPDA
4. Request CDPHE sanitary survey results
5. Attend board meetings — ask for project-by-project status
