# Master Timeline

Chronological record of events. Entries should include sources where possible.

## 2018
- **November** — [[Bill Brunner]] wins CORA lawsuit against Paonia. Judge Schultz finds officials "failed to exercise reasonable diligence." Town pays his legal fees.

## 2024
- **April** — Brunner runs for trustee on transparency platform
- **~May** — Brunner elected with 67.7% (227 votes)
- **August 15** — Board votes 4-1 to remove Brunner after [[Stefen Wynn]] threatens resignation. (Stelter, Czech, Valentine, Hunter for; Swartz against.)

## 2025
- **March 24** — Board tentatively accepts Mile High Accessibility Consultants (MHAC) bid for $33,500 ADA assessment. Lowest of 7 proposals. *Source: High Country Spotlight, March 31, 2025*
- **~April** — Board approves $50,000 Verkada surveillance camera contract. ❓ *Exact date needed*
- **May 25** — [[Samira Vetter]] rejects STR citizen initiative, claiming it "establishes fees" (it eliminated them)
- **June 10** — Board holds executive session to discuss "Initiative Petitions" — possibly illegal under C.R.S. § 24-6-402(4)
- **June 16** — Town posts on Facebook about robot deployment (same day or day before)
- **June 17-18** — Daxbot robots deployed on Paonia streets. Monica Schafer goes live on Facebook. Sheriff's deputy responds with no knowledge. Robots stay ~1 week due to SIM card issues.
- **June 25** — Initiative rejection letter issued
- **July 2** — [[Clay Buchner]] memo with "bankruptcy" doctrine quote
- **July 12** — GJ Sentinel article, Wynn admits Town "caught off guard" by robot arrival
- **September 2025** — Town reports draft ADA policies, grievance procedures developed
- **September 3** — [[Pete McCarthy]] submits six citizen initiatives simultaneously (omnibus submission)

## 2026
- **March** — Initiative v2 drafts completed for [[Camera Ban Initiative]] and [[Robot Moratorium]]
